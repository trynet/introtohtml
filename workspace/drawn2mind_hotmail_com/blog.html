How Much?

by Bud Kraus Published August 13

If you are new to gardening you are going to wonder, "how much should I water, how much should I fertilize"? The truth of the matter is I do it but experience and not by science but that can get me into trouble.

I like to think that i make gardens like the way my Grandmother cooked meals. A pinch of this, a handful of that. Cooking by feel worked for her and it has worked pretty well for me.

Speaking of fertilizer, I try to keep chemicals out of the garden but I am not a purist about this. Once I put way too much fertilizer around the tomato plants and I proved too much is no good either. The overdose resulted in plants that turned yellow and, grew tall and did not yield as many tomatoes as they should have.

Something I'll Pay Attention To Next Year

by Bud Kraus | Published July 12

One thing I've never paid any attention to is the pH factor of my soil. I know this is a measure of a soils acidity and that it has an effect on plants but I've never paid much attention to it since each year I feed my garden beds new soil and other goodies in my preparation .

Here is a table of pH factors I found that will help explain all of this doing some research for this article.

Denomination pH range
Ultra acid 3.5
Extremely acid 3.5-4.4
Very strongly acid 4.5-5.0
Strongly acid 5.1-5.5
Moderately acid 5.6-6.0
Slightly acid 6.1-6.5
Neutral 6.6-7.3
Slightly alkaline 7.4-7.8
Moderately alkaline 7.9-8.4
Strongly alkaline 8.5-9.0
Very Strongly alkaline 9.0
Soil pH

For best results, use a pH factor in the 6 - 7 range
